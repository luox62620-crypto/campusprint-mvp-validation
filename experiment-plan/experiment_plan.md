# CampusPrint Experiment Plan

## Objective

Validate whether UTM KL students are willing to complete a no-queue printing order-flow and show payment/reservation intent before the team builds a full automated printing system.

## Core hypothesis

We believe that UTM KL students living in a selected residence or nearby student accommodation area will complete a simulated online order-flow for no-queue residence/campus pickup printing and show willingness to reserve or pay a small convenience fee.

## Experiment type

- Wizard-of-Oz / simulated order-flow MVP
- Google Form MVP
- Payment-intent test
- Usability feedback survey

## Participants

12 Google Form responses were collected. The core sample prioritized UTM KL residence students and nearby students. Non-residence UTM KL student responses were treated as supporting feedback.

## MVP workflow

1. Participant reads MVP validation consent.
2. Participant identifies their student/residence status.
3. Participant reports campus printing pain point.
4. Participant completes simulated order details.
5. Participant chooses a pickup slot.
6. Participant answers payment/reservation-intent questions.
7. Participant gives usability and improvement feedback.

## Success criteria

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Completed responses | At least 5 |
| Residence / nearby sample | At least 60% |
| Online file submission willingness | At least 60% Yes |
| Payment / reservation intent | At least 60% Yes or Maybe |
| Mock reservation click intent | At least 60% |
| Deadline usage intention | At least 70% Yes or Maybe |
| Ease of use | Average score close to 4/5 |

## Evidence collected

- Google Form screenshots
- Consent screenshot
- Order-flow questions screenshot
- Payment-intent questions screenshot
- Response summary charts
- Anonymized response data
- Evidence analysis spreadsheet

## Decision logic

If order-flow completion and payment/reservation intent are strong, CampusPrint should continue with the no-queue pickup workflow. If usability scores are only moderate, the next version should simplify the form, clarify pickup timing, and strengthen privacy communication.
